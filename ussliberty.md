# Debunking the USS Liberty accident myth

[The Bullshit asymmetry principle, or Brandolini's law](https://www.nature.com/articles/540171a):
> The amount of energy needed to refute bullshit is an order of magnitude bigger than to produce it.

- The notion of IDF intent to attack the US, historians have noted several undisputed facts that demolish such a conspiracy theory: "It is a fact that the air attack on the vessel was halted immediately at the moment of mistaken identity (when the letters were discovered on the ship's side). The same is true in regard to the halting of the torpedo attack. Immediately, the error was discovered, helicopters were dispatched to search for survivors and to aid in the evacuation of the wounded, and two tugs were dispatched from Ashdod to assist the vessel. These actions prove Israel possessed no malicious intentions whatsoever. If she had indeed harbored such intentions, her air and naval forces were clearly in a position to complete their mission and to sink the vessel had they wished.<sup>[[1]](https://web.archive.org/web/20190601071832/https://thelibertyincident.com/docs/israeli/IDF-history-report-en.pdf#page=36)</sup></sup> Further, "Immediately following the incident, Israel indicted her willingness to pay compensation to both the families of the deceased and to the wounded. In June 1968 the Government of Israel paid compensation amounting to $3, 323,000 to the families of the deceased, and in April, 1969, she paid $3, 566,547 to the wounded." Israel also paid, in 1980, $6 million to the US Navy for damage.<sup>[[2]](https://web.archive.org/web/20190601071832/https://thelibertyincident.com/docs/israeli/IDF-history-report-en.pdf#page=35)</sup></sup>

  After the incident, Colonel Ram Ron, tasked with investigating the matter, issued his report finding no wrong-doing or misconduct, but a series of errors. His findings were forwarded to Colonel Meir Shamgar, the Military Advocate General. It was then forwarded to Lieutenant General Itzhak Rabin, Chief of Staff, with a recommendation to convene an adversarial preliminary inquiry under section 283 of the Israel Military Justice Law of 1955. (The equivalent of a U.S. Navy Court of Inquiry.) The MAG wanted to know if there were any individual or individuals against whom charges could be brought. A prosecutor was brought in to make the case of negligence. The Judge's findings, "It is to be noted that throughout the contact no American or any other flag appeared on the ship, and it was only a helicopter, sent after the attack in order to render assistance — if necessary — which  noticed a small American Flag flying over the target. At that stage the vessel was finally identified as an audio-surveillance ship of the US Navy." The USS Liberty "succeeded in leaving the area of the incident under its own power, without requiring assistance offered." The Judge ruled, "I did not discover any deviation from the standard of reasonable behaviour which would justify bringing anyone to trial. Given the above, I hereby decide that there is insufficient prima facie evidence to justify bring any to trial." The MAG studied the report and decided to, "accept the conclusions of the Examining-Judge and not to ask for an indictment.<sup>[[3]](https://web.archive.org/web/20190601071832/https://thelibertyincident.com/docs/israeli/IDF-history-report-en.pdf#page=34)</sup></sup> <sup>[[4]](https://web.archive.org/web/20070905040503/http://www.libertyincident.com/docs/israeli/yerushalmi-report-en.pdf)</sup></sup>

  The conclusions that this incident was a mistake are confirmed in US investigations as well. The 1967 US Navy Court of Inquiry finds, "Available evidence combines to indicate the attack on Liberty on 8 June was in fact a case of mistaken identity. There are no available indications that the attack was intended against a U.S. Ship."<sup>[[5]](https://web.archive.org/web/20070905044632/http://www.libertyincident.com/docs/FindingsOfFact.pdf)</sup></sup> The US Navy Court of Inquiry continues, "We, as well as the Israelis, were apparently deficient in our recognization of competence compared to wwII levels of proficiency. Current US ship profiles are not readily locatable except in such documents as Jane's "fighting ships". Where we have active ship configurations susceptible to confusion with other ships of other nations, we should explore immediately improved identification measures such as national colors on vertical and horizontal surfaces using high visual intensity paints, a safeguard not susceptible to being shot down in the opening stages of an action as was the case in the Liberty incident. Intense fires on the ship coupled with herculean efforts by the engineer department to increase speed both produced heavy black smoke which compounded the recognition problem facing the attacking forces."<sup>[[6]](https://web.archive.org/web/20201111223634/http://www.thelibertyincident.com/docs/CourtOfInquiry.pdf#page=37)</sup></sup> Further, "the foregoing comments by the convening authority authority lead to an overall conclusion that the attack was in fact a mistake."<sup>[[7]](https://libertyincident.com/docs/usncoi/CINCUSNAVEUR-1.pdf)</sup></sup>

  Indeed, CIA's 13 June 1969 assessment is based on NSA SIGINT. It rules out any thesis of the Israeli Navy and Air Force deliberately attacked a ship they knew to be American, "intercepted conversations between the helicopter pilots and the control tower at Hatzor (near Tel Aviv) leave little doubt that the Israelis failed to identify the Liberty as a US ship before or during the attack. Control told (helicopter) 815 at 3:31 p.m. (8:31 a.m.) that "there is a warship there which we attacked. The men jumped into the water from it. You will try to rescue them." Further, "A subsequent message from the control tower to the helicopter identified the ship as Egyptian and told the pilot to return home." The CIA position as of 1978, according to a declassified letter from CIA Director Turner, "It remains our best judgment that the Israeli attack on the USS Liberty was not made in malice toward the United States and was a mistake."<sup>[[8]](https://web.archive.org/web/20070905030705/http://www.libertyincident.com/docs/CIAposition.pdf#page=3)</sup></sup> Historians have ruled, "An examination of the facts in the "Liberty" incident, in their proper context proves beyond any doubt that the attack on the American intelligence ship came about as a result of innocent error by the forces which operated on the spot and the HQs which supervised them."<sup>[[9]](https://web.archive.org/web/20190601071832/https://thelibertyincident.com/docs/israeli/IDF-history-report-en.pdf#page=41)</sup></sup>
